# TechCorp Inc. - Accounting Policies
## Relevant to Month-End Close Reconciliations

---

## General Principles

### Materiality Threshold
- **Quantitative**: Errors/adjustments >$50,000 individually or $150,000 in aggregate are material
- **Qualitative**: Even small amounts may be material if they affect:
  - Compliance with debt covenants
  - Executive compensation metrics
  - Trend analysis (e.g., turning loss to profit)

### Cut-off Procedures
- Transactions recorded in period when goods/services are delivered or received
- Revenue recognized when performance obligations are satisfied
- Expenses recognized when incurred (accrual basis)

---

## Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

### Subscription Revenue
- **Recognition**: Ratably over subscription period (time-based performance obligation)
- **Billing**: Typically annual or multi-year in advance
- **Deferred Revenue**: Cash received but not yet earned is recorded as liability (account 15210)

### Professional Services Revenue
- **Fixed-price projects**: Percentage-of-completion method based on labor hours
- **Time & materials**: Recognized as services are performed
- **Multiple-element arrangements**: Allocate transaction price to separate performance obligations based on standalone selling prices

### Product Revenue (Software Licenses)
- **Perpetual licenses**: Revenue recognized at point of transfer
- **Term licenses**: Ratably over license period

## Expense Accruals

### Accrued Expenses (Account 24100)
**Purpose**: Record expenses incurred but not yet invoiced/paid

**Categories**:
- Vendor services received but not invoiced
- Employee expenses submitted but not reimbursed
- Recurring services where invoice arrives after service period (utilities, cloud services, etc.)

**Accrual Requirements**:
1. Services/goods received in the period
2. Reasonable estimate of amount
3. Supporting documentation (quote, contract, or prior period pattern)
4. Business owner confirmation of completeness

**Reversal Treatment**: Accruals reverse when:
- Invoice is received and processed (invoice hits AP, accrual reverses)
- Payment is made directly from accrual account
- Determination that expense won't be incurred (adjust accrual to zero)

## Intercompany Transactions

### Principles
- All intercompany transactions must reconcile between entities
- Timing differences must be identified and resolved
- Transfer pricing must comply with tax regulations

### Types of IC Transactions
1. **Cost Allocations**: Shared services (IT, facilities, management) allocated based on drivers (headcount, square footage, revenue)
2. **Product Sales**: Transfer pricing for products sold between entities
3. **Management Fees**: Fees for corporate overhead and support
4. **R&D Cost Sharing**: Development costs allocated to entities that benefit

### Reconciliation Requirements
- Monthly reconciliation between US parent and subsidiaries
- Timing differences documented with resolution plan
- Balances confirmed in writing with subsidiary controllers
- FX differences calculated and recorded

## Prepaid Expenses

### Recognition
- Payments made in advance for future benefits recorded as asset (account 13400)
- Amortized over benefit period (typically straight-line)

### Common Prepaid Items
- Software licenses (annual subscriptions)
- Insurance policies (12-36 month policies)
- Maintenance contracts (annual contracts paid upfront)
- Marketing campaigns (paid before campaign launches)

### Amortization
- Monthly amortization schedule maintained for each prepaid item
- Amortization expense recorded to appropriate expense account
- Balance should represent unamortized portion only

## Documentation Requirements (SOX Controls)

### Required Elements for Account Reconciliations
1. **Rollforward**: Beginning balance + Additions - Reductions = Ending balance
2. **Tie to GL**: Ending balance must match general ledger
3. **Variance Explanation**: Material changes vs. prior period explained
4. **Supporting Documentation**: Underlying evidence for amounts (invoices, contracts, confirmations)
5. **Preparer Sign-off**: Prepared by, date
6. **Reviewer Sign-off**: Reviewed by, date, comments
7. **Completeness Assertion**: Evidence that all obligations/assets are recorded

### Risk-Based Documentation Standards
**High-risk accounts** (see chart of accounts) require:
- Detailed rollforward with transaction-level analysis
- Narrative explanation of drivers and unusual items
- Multiple forms of supporting evidence
- Controller-level review

**Medium/Low-risk accounts** require:
- Summary rollforward
- Brief variance explanation
- Key supporting documents
- Manager-level review

## Month-End Close Calendar

### Day 1-2: Data Gathering
- ERP data refresh complete
- Departmental close procedures complete (payroll, sales ops, treasury)
- Data exports available for reconciliation

### Day 3-4: Reconciliation Preparation
- Preparers complete reconciliations
- Submit to reviewer with supporting documentation
- First-pass review by controller

### Day 4-5: Review & Rework
- Controller reviews and provides feedback
- Preparers address comments and resubmit
- Goal: minimize review cycles

### Day 6-8: Final Approval & Close
- Final controller approval
- Executive sign-offs
- Close books in ERP
- Financial statements prepared

## Key Policies for Specific Accounts

### Accrued Expenses (24100)
- Monthly completeness review required (email confirmations from business owners)
- Accruals >$50K require individual documentation and explanation
- Recurring accruals (utilities, cloud services) based on historical average or MTD usage
- Professional services accruals require contract or quote

### Deferred Revenue (15210)
- Contract review required for all new/amended customer contracts
- ASC 606 analysis documented for complex arrangements
- Revenue recognition schedule maintained at contract level
- Reconciliation to billing system required

### Intercompany Receivables/Payables (18200, 28200)
- Monthly confirmation email from subsidiary controllers required
- Timing differences >$100K require investigation and documentation
- Aging analysis prepared monthly
- FX rates documented (source: treasury system)

### Prepaid Expenses (13400)
- Amortization schedule by prepaid item maintained in Excel
- New prepaid items require approval (contract or invoice)
- Monthly review of schedule for accuracy
- Any prepaid item >$100K requires contract attachment

## Audit Considerations

### External Auditor Expectations (per Deloitte)
1. Documentation prepared contemporaneously (not after the fact)
2. Explanations are specific and supported by evidence
3. Unusual items are highlighted and explained
4. Review evidence is clear (not just signature, but substantive comments)
5. Prior period comparisons show trend awareness

### Common Audit Findings (to avoid)
- Missing supporting documentation
- Inadequate variance explanations (too generic)
- Unsupported estimates or judgments
- Untimely preparation or review
- Lack of evidence of completeness procedures
